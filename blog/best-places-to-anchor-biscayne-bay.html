Best Places to Anchor in Biscayne Bay

Biscayne Bay is one of the most beautiful and diverse boating destinations in the United States. Stretching from the shores of downtown Miami to the pristine islands of Biscayne National Park, the bay offers an incredible range of anchoring spots for every type of charter experience. Whether you are looking for a lively sandbar party, a secluded island getaway, or a scenic cruise past some of the most famous waterfront homes in the world, Biscayne Bay has it all. At Miami Yacht Collective, all of our charters depart from Miami Beach Marina, putting you just minutes away from these iconic locations. Our experienced captains know every corner of the bay and will take you to the perfect spot based on your group's vibe and preferences.

Nixon Sandbar (Haulover Sandbar)

If there is one anchoring spot that defines the Miami boating experience, it is the Haulover Sandbar, also commonly known as Nixon Sandbar. Located just north of Bal Harbour between the Intracoastal Waterway and the ocean, this shallow turquoise stretch of water draws hundreds of boats every weekend. The water here is typically waist-deep, crystal clear, and warm, making it the perfect place to hop off the yacht and wade around in the bay. On Saturdays and Sundays the sandbar transforms into a floating party, with music pumping from every direction, vendors selling food and drinks from boat to boat, and a social atmosphere unlike anything else in South Florida.

Even on weekdays, the Haulover Sandbar is a fantastic destination for a private charter. With fewer crowds, you can enjoy the shallow turquoise water in relative peace, take photos, swim, and soak up the sun from the deck. Our captains at Miami Yacht Collective know exactly where to position the yacht for the best experience, whether you want to be in the middle of the action or anchored on the quieter edges of the sandbar. Pair the sandbar stop with one of our catering packages and you have the makings of a perfect Miami day on the water.

Star Island and Hibiscus Island

For guests who want to see how Miami's rich and famous live, a cruise past Star Island and Hibiscus Island is an absolute must. These exclusive man-made islands sit in the heart of Biscayne Bay, connected to the mainland by a single causeway, and are home to some of the most jaw-dropping waterfront mansions in the country. As your yacht glides past, you will see sprawling estates belonging to celebrities, tech moguls, and international business figures. The views of the Miami skyline from this vantage point are equally stunning, especially during golden hour when the downtown skyscrapers light up against the sunset.

While you cannot dock at the private islands themselves, our captains can anchor nearby in calm, protected waters so your group can take in the scenery, snap photos, and enjoy the unique perspective that only a yacht charter provides. This stretch of the bay is a highlight on nearly every Miami Yacht Collective charter, and it pairs beautifully with a sunset cruise or a celebration trip. Whether you are aboard our spacious Ferretti, the versatile Azimut, or the popular Isabella with its included jet ski, Star Island never fails to impress.

Elliot Key and Boca Chita Key

If you are looking to escape the busier waters closer to Miami and experience something truly pristine, Elliot Key and Boca Chita Key inside Biscayne National Park are the destinations for you. Located south of Key Biscayne, these barrier islands offer some of the clearest water in all of South Florida, surrounded by thriving coral reefs and seagrass beds teeming with marine life. Boca Chita Key is famous for its historic ornamental lighthouse and its small harbor, while Elliot Key offers a more rugged, natural shoreline perfect for exploring.

These spots are ideal for longer charters where you have the time to cruise farther south and spend hours snorkeling, swimming, and enjoying the untouched natural beauty. The snorkeling here rivals many Caribbean destinations, with colorful tropical fish, sea turtles, and vibrant coral formations visible in the gin-clear water. Our captains are well-versed in the navigational channels and anchoring regulations within the national park, ensuring a safe and unforgettable experience. For groups booking a full-day charter with Miami Yacht Collective, Elliot Key and Boca Chita Key are among the most rewarding destinations we can offer.

Virginia Key

For guests on shorter charters or those who prefer to stay close to the heart of Miami, Virginia Key is an excellent anchoring spot that is just a short cruise from Miami Beach Marina. Nestled between Key Biscayne and the Rickenbacker Causeway, Virginia Key offers calm, sheltered waters and beautiful views of both the Miami skyline and the lush mangrove shoreline. The beach on the eastern side of the island is scenic and relatively uncrowded compared to South Beach, giving your charter a more relaxed and private feel.

Virginia Key is a favorite among our captains for half-day charters and sunset cruises because of its convenient location and consistently calm conditions. You can anchor in shallow water, swim off the back of the yacht, and enjoy the warm breeze without spending a large portion of your charter time in transit. It is also a great spot for families and smaller groups who want a mellow day on the water. Any yacht in the Miami Yacht Collective fleet, from the intimate Maxum to the spacious Acgua Alberti, is perfectly suited for a Virginia Key afternoon.

Stiltsville

No guide to Biscayne Bay would be complete without mentioning Stiltsville, one of the most unique and photogenic landmarks in all of Miami. Stiltsville is a collection of historic wooden houses built on stilts directly over the shallow waters of the bay, roughly one mile south of Cape Florida on Key Biscayne. Originally built in the 1930s, these structures have survived decades of hurricanes and changing regulations, and today they stand as a beloved symbol of Miami's maritime heritage. The remaining seven houses are maintained by the Stiltsville Trust and are visible from the water, creating a surreal and unforgettable backdrop for your charter.

The waters around Stiltsville are shallow and crystal clear, with a sandy bottom that glows turquoise under the Florida sun. It is an incredible spot for swimming, paddleboarding, and photography. Our captains know exactly how to navigate the shallow flats safely and will position your yacht for the best views and the calmest water. A stop at Stiltsville pairs perfectly with a cruise past Cape Florida Lighthouse and a swing through the deeper channels of Biscayne Bay on the way back to the marina. It is one of those truly only-in-Miami experiences that guests talk about long after the charter ends.

Biscayne Bay is a world-class boating destination, and these five anchoring spots represent just a sample of what is waiting for you on the water. At Miami Yacht Collective, our captains have years of experience navigating the bay and will customize your route to match your group's interests, whether that means a high-energy sandbar party, a serene island escape, or a scenic sunset cruise past Miami's most iconic landmarks. With a fleet that includes everything from the jet-ski-equipped Isabella to the elegant Ferretti, we have the perfect yacht for every occasion.
